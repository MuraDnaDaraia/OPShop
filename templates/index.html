NVIDIA Unveils Game-Changing GeForce RTX 50 Series

Powered by Blackwell Architecture

CES, January 6, 2025 — NVIDIA has officially launched its next-generation GeForce RTX™ 50 Series GPUs for desktops and laptops, ushering in a new era of AI-driven computer graphics. Built on the cutting-edge Blackwell architecture, these GPUs promise unparalleled performance, stunning visual realism, and groundbreaking AI-powered features.

A Leap in Graphics Innovation

The flagship GeForce RTX 5090 boasts 92 billion transistors and delivers over 3,352 trillion AI operations per second (TOPS). It outperforms the GeForce RTX 4090 by up to 2x thanks to innovations like DLSS 4, neural shaders, and next-gen RT Cores.

For gamers looking for value, the GeForce RTX 5070 offers nearly similar performance to the RTX 4090 but costs less than half the price at just $549, compared to the RTX 4090’s $1,599 price tag.

DLSS 4: AI-Powered Performance Boost

The new DLSS 4 brings Multi Frame Generation, using AI to generate up to three frames for every traditionally rendered frame. This boosts performance by up to 8x, while ensuring responsiveness with NVIDIA Reflex 2. Reflex 2 introduces Frame Warp, reducing latency by up to 75% for competitive gaming.

DLSS 4 also debuts the transformer model architecture, delivering greater stability, reduced ghosting, and higher detail, with support for over 75 games and applications at launch.

Blackwell’s AI Revolution: Neural Shaders and Realistic Characters

NVIDIA introduces RTX Neural Shaders, which use small AI networks to deliver film-quality materials and lighting in real-time games.

Rendering lifelike characters gets a massive upgrade with RTX Neural Faces, enabling real-time generation of ultra-realistic digital faces. Coupled with ray-traced hair and skin, and RTX Mega Geometry, which enables 100x more ray-traced triangles, games will achieve unprecedented realism.

Autonomous Game Characters with NVIDIA ACE

Blackwell GPUs bring autonomous game characters to life using NVIDIA ACE technologies. These AI-driven characters adapt to player behavior, plan strategies, and act like real humans.

  • In PUBG: BATTLEGROUNDS, ACE-powered companions dynamically assist human players.
  • In InZOI, characters autonomously adjust their behavior based on in-game events and goals.
  • In MIR5, AI-powered raid bosses adapt tactics to challenge players dynamically.

AI Tools for Creators and Developers

The RTX 50 Series empowers creators with FP4 precision, doubling AI image generation performance for tools like FLUX and enabling local generative AI models to run more efficiently.

NVIDIA also introduces NIM microservices for developers, enabling AI-powered assistants and agents on RTX PCs. For creators, the NVIDIA Broadcast app gains new AI tools like Studio Voice for better audio and Virtual Key Light for optimized lighting.

Pricing and Availability

  • GeForce RTX 5090: $1,999 (Jan. 30)
  • GeForce RTX 5080: $999 (Jan. 30)
  • GeForce RTX 5070 Ti: $749 (Feb.)
  • GeForce RTX 5070: $549 (Feb.)

Laptops with RTX 50 Series GPUs will launch in March (RTX 5090/5080/5070 Ti) and April (RTX 5070), from brands like ASUS, Acer, Dell, Lenovo, Razer, and more.

The Bottom Line

The GeForce RTX 50 Series represents the future of gaming and creative workflows, combining cutting-edge AI, unmatched performance, and revolutionary features. Whether you're a gamer, creator, or developer, NVIDIA's Blackwell-powered GPUs deliver groundbreaking performance and value, with the RTX 5070 offering high-end performance at a fraction of the cost of the RTX 4090.
